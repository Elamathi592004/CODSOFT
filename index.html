Literary Achievements

  • Mahakavi (Great Poet): Bharathi is often referred to as "Mahakavi," meaning "Great Poet," in recognition of his profound contributions to Tamil literature.
  • Innovative Poetry: He introduced modern prose-poetry into Tamil literature and used simple yet powerful language to convey deep messages. His works include patriotic songs, devotional hymns, and reflective poetry.
  • Pioneering Journalism: Bharathi edited and contributed to several notable journals such as "India" and "Bala Bharatham," using them as platforms to express his revolutionary ideas and inspire the masses.
  • Influence on Tamil Literature: His works significantly impacted the Tamil literary world, influencing subsequent generations of writers and poets.

Contributions to Indian Independence Movement

  • Patriotic Songs: Bharathi's patriotic songs, such as "Vande Mataram," "Entha Kavi Paadinalum," and "Achamillai Achamillai," played a crucial role in inspiring the Indian populace to join the struggle for independence.
  • Social Reforms: He advocated for the upliftment of women and the eradication of caste discrimination. His writings often addressed these social issues, promoting equality and justice.
  • Association with Freedom Fighters: Bharathi was closely associated with other prominent freedom fighters, including Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Lala Lajpat Rai, and Aurobindo Ghosh. His revolutionary ideas and active participation in the freedom struggle made him a significant figure in the movement.
  • Exile and Imprisonment: Due to his revolutionary activities and writings, he faced persecution from the British authorities. He spent some years in exile in Pondicherry (then a French colony) and was imprisoned for his nationalist activities.
